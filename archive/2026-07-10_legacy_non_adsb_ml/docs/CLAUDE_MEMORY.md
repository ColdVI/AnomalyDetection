**What was built (three independent, parallel efforts, one per architecture, "Kol L/D/U" = "arm L/D/U" of ML-16):**

- **Kol D (Dense-AE):** new `src/ml/models/dense_autoencoder.py` (flat feed-forward autoencoder, same window/feature config as LSTM for fair comparison), `scripts/run_ml_dense_ae_sead_evaluation.py`, ön-kayıt `docs/ML16_KOL_D_DENSE_AE_SEAD_PLAN.md`, tests `tests/test_dense_ae_sead_integration.py`. - **Kol U (USAD):** new `src/ml/models/usad.py` (shared-encoder two-decoder adversarial autoencoder per Audibert et al. 2020), `scripts/run_ml_usad_sead_evaluation.py`, ön-kayıt `docs/ML16_KOL_U_USAD_SEAD_PLAN.md`, tests `tests/test_usad_sead_integration.py`. Result: `docs/decisions.md` ADR-018.

Each registered exactly 3 score variants before running anything (recon-alone, recon+ml14_fusion max-fused, recon+itki_komutu max-fused) against the SAME frozen operational targets (critical ≥0.30 recall @ ≤2 FA/h; advisory ≥0.50 recall @ ≤12 FA/h — never changed).

**Results — ALL THREE FAILED Gate B (operational target).** Gate A (holdout isolation + determinism: recomputed existing_fusion/itki_komutu/ml14_fusion columns must exactly match frozen `artifacts/ml14/uav_sead/full_matrix` CSVs) PASSED for all three, 90/90 rows, max_abs_diff ~7e-15. Best raw numbers (5-seed mean, threshold/critical decision): LSTM 0.219 recall @ 2.92 FA/h, Dense-AE 0.215 @ 2.77, USAD 0.217 @ 2.87 — all roughly DOUBLE the current best (`ml14_fusion` critical 0.043 recall) but none inside the FA budget, and — critically — see next paragraph before treating this as a real win.

**THE KEY FINDING (do not lose this — it is more important than the recall numbers):** an independent cross-model consistency check found that all three architecturally different models produced near-identical per-category detected-event counts under the `threshold` decision on split_00 (e.g. `external_position_anomaly` 70/185 detected in all three). This is NOT plausible as "all three learned equally well." Root cause, verified directly (not just asserted) and saved at `artifacts/ml_lstm_sead/uav_sead/full_matrix/magnitude_domination_diagnostic.json`: the trained LSTM's score ranking is Spearman ρ=0.964 correlated with a COMPLETELY UNTRAINED random-init network of the same architecture, and ρ=0.965 with a model-free `‖x‖²` raw-magnitude baseline — training adds almost nothing beyond "how large is the scaled input." The project-wide `RobustScaler` (unmodified, used everywhere) does not clip outliers, so a handful of extreme-magnitude windows — genuine GPS-spoofing jumps in `external_position_anomaly` flights, plus at least one flight LABELED "normal" (`2018-05-26/16_50_55`) that actually has a frozen-GPS/`eph`≈25000 sentinel artifact (gps_frozen_count~700+, almost certainly a mislabeled or data-quality-corrupted flight, not investigated further, flagged for later) — dominate reconstruction error for ANY bounded-output autoencoder regardless of architecture. `ThresholdPolicy` itself was directly tested and is NOT degenerate (only 4.3% of alarms coincide with first-available-score timing; mean onset t=80s vs mean first-finite t=6.8s) — this rules out a decision-layer bug. **Conclusion: none of the three models' "doubled recall" numbers should be presented as "the model learned meaningful temporal/reconstruction signal" — they are substantially a raw-magnitude-outlier artifact of unclipped scaling, shared by all three architectures.** This is documented as prominently as the numbers themselves in ADR-016/017/018 and `docs/ML_YETERSIZLIKLER_KAYDI.md` (open item B.5, and C.1/C.3 updated). Per project discipline, scaling/features were NOT changed post-hoc after seeing this — it's logged as an open item for a future, newly pre-registered round (candidate fix: clip/robust-scale before scoring, or a magnitude-normalized reconstruction score).
